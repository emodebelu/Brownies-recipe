Peanut Butter Brownies

Peanut butter brownies are a delicious dessert that combines the rich, chocolatey goodness of brownies with the nutty, creamy flavor of peanut butter. These decadent treats are typically made by mixing peanut butter into the brownie batter, creating a swirl of peanut butter throughout the chocolatey goodness.

The exact recipe for peanut butter brownies can vary depending on personal preferences and baking techniques. Some recipes call for the addition of chopped peanuts to the batter for added texture, while others might use a combination of creamy and crunchy peanut butter for a contrast in texture.

Peanut butter brownies can be enjoyed warm or cold, and are often served with a cold glass of milk or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. They are a popular dessert choice for those who love the combination of sweet and salty flavors, and are a perfect treat for any occasion, from family gatherings to holiday parties.

Whether you're a fan of peanut butter, brownies, or both, you're sure to love the rich, decadent flavor of peanut butter brownies. So why not give this delicious dessert a try and indulge in a sweet treat that's sure to satisfy your cravings?

Here's a recipe for Peanut Butter Brownies:

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup light brown sugar, packed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9-inch square baking pan with cooking spray or butter
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the melted butter, granulated sugar, and light brown sugar until well combined.
  3. Add the eggs and vanilla extract to the bowl and whisk until smooth.
  4. In a separate mixing bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, and salt.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan, spreading it evenly.
  7. Drop spoonfuls of peanut butter onto the batter, then swirl the peanut butter into the batter with a butter knife or toothpick.
  8. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the brownies are set and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  9. Let the brownies cool for 10 minutes in the pan before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.
  10. Cut into squares and serve. Enjoy!

These peanut butter brownies are perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth, and the combination of chocolate and peanut butter is always a crowd-pleaser.
